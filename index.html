Career Search

Part 1 - Job opportunities

Career I chose: Systems Analyst

Systems analysts are required to implement, maintain, and support IT and information systems. They analyze an organization’s current infrastructure and meet the needs of the organization, scaling up systems as they grow. Systems analysts are usually not directly involved in software or hardware development.

Education Required

Most systems analysts are required to have a Bachelor's degree in the field of computer science, information technology, engineering, or information systems. Good analytical skills and creative problem solving skills are necessary for success in the field.

Job Outlook

The job outlook of systems analysts is projected to grow 7 percent from 2019 to 2029, which is a higher rate than most other occupations. The increase in demand for IT services in healthcare and the adoption of cloud computing by businesses is placing a high demand for systems analysts.

The average salary for systems analysts is $70 427 a year in Canada.

Why am I interested in this career?

I would be interested in this career because of a few reasons. First of all, I have good analytical and problem solving skills which are required for this career. Second, I am interested in networks and computer systems and I think I would be good at designing infrastructure for businesses. Finally, I think I would like to work alongside programmers and hardware engineers and to innovate and apply new technologies and ideas to computer systems.

Part 2 - University Program

This program to obtain a Master of Digital Transformation and Innovation is provided by uOttawa.

Entrance Requirements

To be eligible, candidates must:

  • Have a Bachelor’s degree with a specialization or a major (or equivalent) in a relevant discipline with a minimum admission average of 75% (B+).
    Note: International candidates must check the admission equivalencies for the diploma they received in their country of origin.
  • Have completed a course in statistics; a course in management information systems or computing; and two advanced courses showing specialization in one of three disciplines: creative arts and humanities (online multi-media or communications); management (digital transformation management or strategy); or technology (online application development or data science).

What a first-year program would be like

The first component of the degree program is the introductory seminar. A research workshop is necessary as well. Then, several compulsory courses are necessary, including Communication Ethics, Ethics for Design, AI, and Robotics, and Ethics, Values and Information Dilemmas.

Other things required to completethe program

Several compulsory courses as well as a research project are necessary in order to complete the program.

This program takes up to 2 years with full-time study.
